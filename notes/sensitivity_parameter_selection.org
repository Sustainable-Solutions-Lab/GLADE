* Parameter selection for global sensitivity analysis

Notes on which parameters to include in the PCE-based global sensitivity
analysis, based on preliminary scenario sweeps (configs =current_yields= and
=prod_stability=).

** Food utility meta-parameters — not relevant

We swept the piecewise utility parameters =decline_factor= ∈ {0.5, 0.7, 0.9}
and =n_blocks= ∈ {2, 4, 8} in a 3×3 grid, both with and without health/GHG
pricing. Global average diets are nearly identical across all 9 combinations in
both pricing regimes. The model is robust to these meta-parameters; they do not
need to be included in the sensitivity analysis.

** Production stability L1 penalty — potentially relevant

We swept =l1_cost= across {0.01, 0.05, 0.1, 0.5, 1, 5, 10, 50, 100} in two
pricing regimes: (yll=5000, ghg=200) and (yll=0, ghg=0).

- *With health/GHG pricing*: Diets are stable across the full range of L1
  costs. The pricing signals dominate the solution structure.
- *Without pricing*: L1 cost has a strong effect on diet composition. Low
  values allow the optimizer to deviate freely from baseline production; high
  values lock in current patterns.

This suggests L1 cost interacts with the pricing parameters and could be worth
including, at least as a nuisance parameter or to verify it doesn't confound the
main effects.

** Current PCE sensitivity parameters

The =pce_sensitivity_scenarios.yaml= config currently includes the following
parameters. We haven't done dedicated sweeps for most of these; they were chosen
based on prior expectations about what drives model uncertainty.

*Emission factors* (currently active; see =docs/sensitivity_analysis.rst= for
full justification with references):
- =ch4_factor= [0.5, 1.5]: ±50%, representing combined EF + GWP100
  uncertainty. EF uncertainty ±40% (midpoint of IPCC Tier 1 ±30–50%)
  and GWP100 uncertainty ±40% (IPCC AR6, 90% CI) combine in quadrature
  to ±57%, rounded conservatively to ±50%.
- =n2o_factor= [0.3, 1.7]: ±70%, representing combined EF + GWP100
  uncertainty. EF uncertainty ±50% (Tian et al. 2020 global aggregate
  ×0.66–1.53; structural uncertainty from aggregated EFs) and GWP100
  uncertainty ±47% (IPCC AR6, 90% CI) combine in quadrature to ±69%,
  rounded to ±70%.
- =luc_factor= [0.3, 1.7]: ±70%, matching IPCC AR6 WGIII 90% CI for
  CO2-LULUCF emissions. Supported by Global Carbon Budget CV ≈ 58% (1σ),
  AGB uncertainty ~50%, and SOC factor uncertainty ~20–25%.

*Health relative risk quantiles* (currently active):
- =rr_whole_grains= [0, 1]: Interpolates between GBD low/high RR estimates.
- =rr_legumes= [0, 1]: Same.
- =rr_red_meat= [0, 1]: Same.

*Valuation / slice parameters* (currently active):
- =value_per_yll= [0, 20000]: YLL monetary valuation (policy choice).
- =ghg_price= [0, 300]: Carbon price (policy choice).

*Not currently included*:
- =yield_factor= [0.8, 1.2]: Global crop yield uncertainty (±20%). Well-
  supported by grid-cell NRMSE (18–28%) and inter-annual CV (13–22% for major
  cereals). Excluded to reduce dimensionality.
- =rr_fruits=, =rr_vegetables=, =rr_nuts_seeds=: Additional health risk
  factors. Left out to keep dimensionality manageable; these food groups may
  be less decision-relevant than grains/legumes/red meat.

** Open questions

- Should =l1_cost= be added as a parameter? It's not a physical uncertainty but
  a modelling choice; including it would test robustness to this assumption.
- The =total_width_multiplier= for food utility was not tested (held at 2.0).
  Given the insensitivity to the other utility parameters, this is likely fine.
